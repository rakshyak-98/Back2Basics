IPTV stands for **Internet Protocol Television**. It's a system for delivering television content over internet networks (IP-based) instead of traditional broadcast, satellite, or cable methods.

**How it works:**

- Video streams are sent via IP packets over internet/network connections
- Users access content through set-top boxes, smart TVs, computers, or mobile apps
- Streams are typically unicast (direct to individual users) or multicast (one stream to multiple users)

**Common delivery models:**

- **Live TV**: Real-time broadcast channels (news, sports, events)
- **Video on Demand (VOD)**: Pre-recorded content users select and watch anytime
- **Time-shifted TV**: Replay of recent broadcasts

**Typical architecture:**

- Streaming server (like Flussonic) encodes and delivers video
- Content management system organizes channels/content
- Set-top box or app receives and decodes stream
- **[[CAS (Conditional Access System)]]** (operator STBs) or **[[DRM]]** (OTT apps) enforces subscriber entitlements
- User interface (EPG - Electronic Program Guide) for navigation

**Advantages:**

- On-demand viewing flexibility
- Lower infrastructure cost than traditional broadcast
- Can be delivered over any IP network (internet, private network, LAN)

**Common uses:**

- Residential TV services (replacing cable)
- Corporate/closed-network broadcasting
- Live event streaming
- Media distribution in hotels, hospitals, venues
